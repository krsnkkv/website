Chamber 01 — Dossier
About

Clearance Granted — Personal File

Final-year MEng Computer Science & Software Engineering student at the University of Birmingham, completing my degree at the Edgbaston campus after three years at Birmingham's Dubai campus. Predicted First Class.

My dissertation — How Do We Hear a Rāga? — is a six-stage interpretable ML pipeline for Carnatic rāga classification. It uses pitch contour extraction, tonic normalisation, swara mapping, and transition-based features to classify rāgas the way a trained listener might.

Outside research, I co-founded Rahla (AR travel app, App Olympics semi-finalist), lead creative and operations at Nova Studio (indie game studio), and delivered "Can AI Dream?" — a public workshop on AI creativity for the UAE AI Summer Camp.

The long-term thread: I believe cognition should constrain and validate computational design. Music is my entry point, not the destination.
